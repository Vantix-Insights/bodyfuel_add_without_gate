Workout-Day vs. Rest-Day Macros

Carb cycling — more carbs on training days, fewer on rest days — improves body composition without changing your weekly calorie average. Most people eat the same every day and leave results on the table.

Day TypeCaloriesProteinCarbsFat
Training DayTDEE +0 to +100HighHighModerate
Rest DayTDEE −150 to −200HighLowHigher
Example at 2,200 kcal TDEE: Training day = 2,250 kcal (280g carbs, 170g protein, 55g fat). Rest day = 2,000 kcal (160g carbs, 170g protein, 75g fat). Protein never changes.

Meal Timing Blueprint

Timing is a secondary factor — but costs nothing to implement and adds a measurable edge on top of your calorie and macro targets.

WindowWhat to eatWhy
On waking (30 min)20–40g protein (eggs, yoghurt, shake)Stops overnight muscle breakdown immediately
Pre-workout (1–2 hrs)Carbs + moderate protein, low fatCarbs fuel intensity; fat slows digestion
Post-workout (within 2 hrs)Carbs + protein, 3:1 ratioReplenishes glycogen, triggers muscle synthesis
Last mealSlow protein (cottage cheese) + vegetablesCasein feeds muscles during overnight fast
Optional before bed20g casein or 200g cottage cheeseIncreases overnight muscle synthesis ~22%
One rule that matters most: Eat your biggest carb meals around training. Eat highest fat meals away from training. That shift alone improves body composition without touching total calories.

Plateau-Breaker Protocol

A plateau = no weight movement in 14 consecutive days despite following your plan. Your metabolism adapted. Here's how to break it without destroying your metabolism.

StepActionDuration
1 — VerifyWeigh your food for 3 days. Most "plateaus" are tracking errors.3 days
2 — Diet breakEat at TDEE (maintenance) for 7–14 days. Resets leptin and metabolic rate.1–2 weeks
3 — Reduce furtherAfter break, reduce by 100–150 kcal and resume.Until next plateau
4 — Increase NEATAdd 3,000–5,000 extra steps daily. Preserves muscle better than cutting food.Ongoing
5 — Weekly refeedOnce per week eat at TDEE with +100–150g extra carbs.Weekly
Never drop below 1,200 kcal. Causes muscle loss, nutrient deficiencies, hormonal damage, and permanently lowers your maintenance calorie level. Always break plateaus upward first.

Sleep & Recovery Nutrition

Sleep deprivation raises ghrelin (hunger) by 28% and lowers leptin (fullness) by 18%. You wake measurably hungrier and your body resists fat loss harder after poor sleep.

SleepFat loss effectMuscle effect
Under 6 hrsFat loss reduced ~55%60% of weight lost is muscle
7–9 hrsOptimal environmentNormal protein synthesis
Before bed: 20–40g casein protein (cottage cheese is best). Proven to increase overnight muscle protein synthesis by ~22%. Avoid high sugar, alcohol, and large fatty meals before sleep.
